**An EdCounsellor**
The Overseas Education Counsellor is a vital support person for students, particularly international students, in the higher education sector, offering guidance and assistance in various areas. They are typically introduced as a middle person who provides assistance and support in academic and psychological aspects. In many educational environments, the role of the student counsellor initially exists as a form of student adviser or admissions officer in the student support unit.

In the context of the higher education industry, the student adviser’s role stems and emerges directly from the representation rights granted by the affiliated higher education institutions. The adviser typically serves as a host to guide students through educational and career progression, often acting as a bridge between students and the institutions, helping them navigate complex admission and enrolment processes, adjust to a new environment, and connect with applicable services and systems, establishing trust.

From the time a student arrives in an educational institution to explore and learn about overseas education options, the student adviser/admission officer often engages in supporting the student through orientation programs, responding to personal questions, educating to enhance student life on campus, as well as ensuring that all open-ended, readily available online resources and essential information are offered without the need for any unnecessary charge. However, the ultimate goal of the adviser is to grant the student a sense of feeling that they will indeed be safe and belong, without any second thoughts, once they arrive at their destination.

Extending beyond the advisory/admission role, at FutureWise Counselling, the counsellor proceeds further to offer two distinct services from an end-to-end perspective: Psychological and Academic profiling. These two services are provided after a needs analysis session with the student, if the student decides to sign up with the counsellor as their personal overseas education counsellor. Here, the counsellor provides individual student support while recognising the student transitions that can occur due to changes to their personal academic, lifestyle and socio-cultural contexts.

In a merger of the student advisory/admission and overseas education consulting roles, plus mentoring, the Personal Student Counsellor role emerges, and it is both reactive: based on student inputs, and proactive: recognising student needs and developing personalised programmes based on those needs that enrich student experience and future potential; thus, creating both presence and guidance in a student’s overall journey.
